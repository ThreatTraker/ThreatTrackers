Types of Malware

Computer Virus

A computer virus is a type of malicious software (malware) that is designed to infect computer systems and spread from one device to another.


Worm

A worm virus, often referred to simply as a worm, is a self-replicate virus and spread across computer networks without requiring user interaction.


Trojan Horse

A Trojan horse virus is a type of malware that disguises itself as legitimate software or files, tricking users into unknowingly installing it.


Spyware

Spyware virus is a type of malware that secretly collects and monitors user activities, such as browsing habits, and personal information, often without the user's consent.


Adware

Adware virus is a type of malware that displays unwanted advertisements, often in the form of pop-ups or banners, to generate revenue for the attacker and disrupt the user's browsing experience.


Rootkit

A rootkit virus is a type of malware that enables unauthorized access and control of a computer system while hiding its presence and compromising the system's security.


Example of Reason Why Malware is Dangerous?

Data theft

Malware can infiltrate a system and steal sensitive information such as personal data, financial details, login credentials, or intellectual property. This stolen information can then be exploited for various malicious purposes, including identity theft or financial fraud.

Unauthorized access

Certain types of malware, such as remote access trojans (RATs), can grant unauthorized access to an infected device. This can enable hackers to gain control over the compromised system, monitor activities, install additional malware, or use it as a launchpad for further cyberattacks.

Ransomware attacks

Ransomware is a specific type of malware that encrypts files on a victim's system, making them inaccessible until a ransom is paid. This form of malware has caused significant financial losses for individuals, businesses, and even critical infrastructure providers, disrupting operations and causing widespread damage.

5 Steps How to Avoid Malware

Step 1

Use Reliable Antivirus Software

Install reputable antivirus software on your devices and keep it up to date. Antivirus software can detect and block known malware, as well as provide real-time protection against new threats.

Step 2

Keep Your Software Updated

Regularly update your operating system, web browsers, plugins, and other software applications. Software updates often include security patches that address vulnerabilities that malware can exploit. Enable automatic updates whenever possible to ensure you stay protected.

Step 3

Exercise Caution with Email and Downloads

Be cautious when opening email attachments or downloading files from the internet. Avoid opening emails or attachments from unknown senders, as they may contain malware. Only download files from trusted sources, such as official websites, and verify their authenticity before opening or executing them.

Step 4

Enable Firewall Protection

Enable the built-in firewall on your operating system or install a reputable third-party firewall. Firewalls act as a barrier between your device and the internet, monitoring incoming and outgoing network traffic and blocking suspicious or malicious connections.

Step 5

Practice Safe Browsing Habits

Be mindful of the websites you visit and the links you click on. Avoid visiting suspicious or untrustworthy websites, especially those that host illegal or pirated content. Be cautious when clicking on pop-up ads, as they can sometimes lead to malware-infected websites. Additionally, consider using a reputable ad-blocker extension to minimize the risk of accidental clicks on malicious advertisements.
